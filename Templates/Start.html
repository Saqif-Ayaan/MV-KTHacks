What is a tumor?

A tumor is a growth of cells that diverges from regular cell division.
The tumor overides cellular regulation due to a mutation of the gene from damaged cells.
While some tumors can be benign, many are either cancerous or evolve to become cancerous. https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/21881-tumor


How does a tumor form?

Damaged genes are able to lead in inhibited regulation of cellular division.
As a result, many "imperfect" cells can begin to form, mutating further and further as they rapidly divide.
Stress, smoking, sun exposure, unhealthy foods, and alcohol are all leading factors of cell damage
and, as a result, contribute to someone's likelihood of developing the disease. https://www.verywellhealth.com/cancer-causes-513773


Metastasis

As cancer divides, it has the ability to spread to other organs; this process is frequently referred to as metastasis.
The significance of metastasis is that, depending on the location of the cancer, it may impair a different bodily function.
Furthermore, the location of the primary site—where cancer had originally developed—plays a role in determining what type of cancer is present.
The type of cancer can then be used to determine proper treatment options, as well as, possible developments in the cancer and metastasis.
With this, to completely treat and eliminate cancer, it is vital to track down this primary site, which is what our project seeks to accomplish. https://training.seer.cancer.gov/disease/diagnosis/
